§ about me

the curious mind
behind the research.

Hi!! My name is Stephanie Zhu. I am a Biochemistry and Legal Studies student at the University of Wisconsin–Madison, drawn to the overlap between science, policy, and aesthetics.

My academic focus spans skincare regulation, biotech intellectual property, and visual culture. I'm drawn to questions about how regulatory frameworks shape innovation, who gets to patent beauty, and what the legal landscape looks like for the next generation of cosmetic science.

I came to this work through genuine curiosity, and I'm building the research and legal literacy to one day contribute meaningfully to it.

§ ongoing project

Skincare Patent
Database

An ongoing independent research project tracking active ingredient patents, regulatory approval timelines, and innovation trends across the U.S. and Korean skincare markets.

I'm building a personal database of cosmetic ingredient patents filed with the USPTO and KIPO, cross-referenced against FDA and MFDS approval records. The goal is to identify where regulatory lag is slowing down innovation — and to understand what the legal landscape looks like for a future biotech patent attorney.
